# Declaration of generative-AI use

> Most journals (and most ethics policies) now require an explicit statement on
> the use of generative AI in preparing a manuscript. Because this paper is itself
> about AI integrity, a precise, candid statement matters more than usual. Edit the
> bracketed parts to reflect exactly what you did — under-claiming or vague wording
> reads worse than an honest account.

## Statement for the manuscript

During the preparation of this work, the author(s) used [[TOOL NAME(S) AND
VERSION]] in order to [[state the purpose — e.g. "improve the language and
readability of the manuscript" and/or "assist with code drafting and debugging in
the analysis pipeline"]]. After using these tools/services, the author(s) reviewed
and edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of
the publication.

Generative AI was **not** used to generate, fabricate, or alter any data, results,
figures, or tables. All quantitative results are produced by the deterministic
analysis pipeline described in the manuscript and released in the project
repository. The commercial AI assistants named in the study (Anthropic Claude,
OpenAI GPT/ChatGPT, Google Gemini, xAI Grok, DeepSeek, Perplexity) are **objects of
study** — they are queried only through the documented collection harness to elicit
the multiple-choice questions under analysis — and were not used to author the
scientific claims of the paper.

## Notes for completing this statement

- Name each tool and, where you can, the version (e.g. "Claude Opus 4.x",
  "ChatGPT (GPT-4-class)"). "An AI assistant" is acceptable if a version is
  genuinely unknown, but be specific where you can.
- Distinguish clearly between (a) AI as a *writing/coding aid* for the paper and
  (b) AI as the *subject under measurement*. The two must not be conflated.
- If you used AI only as a study subject and not as a writing aid, say so plainly:
  "Generative AI was used solely as the object of study (to elicit the MCQs
  analyzed herein) and was not used in the writing or analysis."
- Generative-AI tools must not be listed as authors. Authorship requires
  accountability that a tool cannot hold.
- Check the target journal's exact policy wording (Elsevier, Springer Nature,
  IEEE, ACM, COPE all publish their own templates) and match it; the paragraph
  above follows the common Elsevier/COPE phrasing.
